Supply must equal demand every second. There is no large-scale warehouse for electricity.

First Response (grid operators call this FCR)

The instant frequency drops, batteries and power-plant control systems respond automatically within seconds. Their job: arrest the fall and buy time. No human intervention — pure physics and control loops.

Secondary Response (called aFRR)

Within 30 seconds, fast-ramping plants kick in, such as pumped hydro and gas turbines. They restore frequency to 50.00 Hz and free the batteries for the next event.

Backup & Relief (called mFRR)

Operators call on standby gas plants, factories that cut back, and cross-border cables to relieve the secondary reserves and rebuild margin. This can take 10–15 minutes.

Markets, trading, and prices must operate within this physical reality of a real-time balance between supply and demand.
