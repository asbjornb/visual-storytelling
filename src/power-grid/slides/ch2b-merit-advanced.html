The same auction, but with many individual power plants and a demand that responds to price.

Each thin bar is one generating unit. Costs vary within the same technology — an old coal plant costs more than a new one; a wind farm in Jutland bids differently from one in Bavaria.

The demand curve now slopes downward: at higher prices, large industrial consumers reduce load. Where supply meets demand is the equilibrium.

Must-run plants (nuclear, some renewables) cannot easily shut down. They bid at or below zero to guarantee dispatch — even if it means negative prices.
