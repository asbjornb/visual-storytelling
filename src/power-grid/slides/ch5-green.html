Watch how the generation mix shifts through a single day. At noon, solar floods the grid. At sunset, it vanishes — and gas must ramp violently.

Crank up solar capacity: midday becomes a wall of yellow. Now add batteries — they charge on cheap midday solar (bars above the demand line) and discharge at sunset to displace expensive gas peakers (green).

Battery owners forecast the day and spread charge/discharge to maximise the price spread: buy cheap, sell dear. The grid gets cleaner and prices stabilise.

The green transition makes the grid more volatile, not less. Batteries are the shock absorbers — charging on surplus renewables, discharging to shave peaks.
