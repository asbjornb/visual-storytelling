Grid Pressures

The grid usually looks calm. You only understand it when it’s under stress — when prices explode, operators scramble, and the limits of physics become visible.

The Wind Dies Down

Forecast said 15 GW offshore wind. Reality: 8 GW. Batteries fire first, then gas turbines spin up. Norway opens hydro south. Intraday prices spike as operators and traders scramble to close the gap within minutes to hours.

Outcome
Peak price€175/MWh
WhereGermany
Duration4–8 hours

Who paidConsumers via day-ahead price
LessonForecast errors hit fast. Batteries and gas reserves buy time.

The Nordic Dry Spell

Low rainfall empties Scandinavian reservoirs over weeks. Norway conserves water. Gas fills the gap across the continent. Unlike sudden crises, this persists for weeks or months — long enough for traders to hedge and politics to intervene.

Outcome
Peak price€230/MWh
WhereGermany
DurationWeeks to months
Who actedTraders, importers, then governments
Who paidEveryone — slow burn across the continent
LessonEnergy scarcity grinds. Markets adapt, but consumers feel it for months.

The French Nuclear Surprise

Stress corrosion sidelines 30 of 56 French reactors. France flips from top exporter to desperate importer. Prices exceed €420/MWh. The shockwave spreads continent-wide and lasts months while reactors are inspected and repaired.

Outcome
Peak price€420/MWh
WhereFrance, then everywhere
Duration6+ months
Who actedEveryone: operators, traders, governments, EU
Who paidFrench consumers; continent via price coupling
LessonStructural outages reshape markets. No quick fix when baseload vanishes.
